\subsection{Interpretation}\label{interpretation}

Regression 1 shows that holding unemployment rate and health expenditure
constant, a one percent increase in GDP per capita is associated with
1.555 additional years of life expectancy. This result is statistically
significant at the 1\% level. Unemployment rate is not statistically
significant. Health expenditure is positive and statistically
significant.

Regression 2 is the preferred regression. It filters for countries with
above-average GDP per capita and adds an interaction term between log
GDP and health expenditure. The interaction term is positive and
significant at the 5\% level, suggesting that in wealthier countries,
the combined effect of GDP and health spending is stronger. This is the
preferred regression.
